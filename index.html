Chapter 1 — How we met

That one wrong message

I joined the new school in 2024. New class, new faces, nothing saved in my phone. A math group got made and I was added. I wanted to message Adarsh. Instead I messaged you. Yep. I texted a girl thinking she was a guy from class. You replied, told me you were not Adarsh, and then refused to tell your name. Classic start.

If that message had not happened, we honestly might never have talked properly. That single mistake turned into late messages about studies, random jokes, and finally, by Diwali 2024, you told me your name — Niharika. October 2024 is when we became proper friends. Funny how life sets small traps that turn into good things.

Chapter 2 — First impressions

Cool, focused, and actually scary at studies

First real impression: you are mad serious about studies. Not just smart, but consistent. Watching you study made me want to stop sleeping on notes. You were the one who actually cared about the paper, the one who prepared properly. Lowkey motivation material.

You are talented and hardworking. You help others, explain doubts, and somehow make complex things simple. That gave me confidence and help when I needed it. Thanks for that.

Chapter 3 — Nicknames & personalities

Ms Slayy, Pookie, Temdi, Malich — same person, different chaos

You have so many modes and honestly I love exposing each one. Ms Slayy is the confident one. Pookie is the soft one who actually checks on people. Temdi is full chaos, random nonsense energy that derails conversations. Malich is stubborn and won't give up once you pick a side.

Also you call me suvar and kukur and I still don't know if I should be offended or grateful. Balance, right?

Chapter 4 — Funny moments

Small things that keep replaying

We don't need big events to laugh. Half the time we begin with a serious question and five minutes later we are talking nonsense. You say something random, I try to be serious, and the whole chat collapses into jokes. There was that one time I tried to ask something and you replied with a line so random I still remember it and laugh.

Another classic: when you get annoyed, you explain with full energy and the serious face makes it even funnier. You try to act calm when people laugh and fail spectacularly. Those moments are golden.

Chapter 6 — Things I admire

You are brave in small ways. You keep moving even when things get heavy. Real bravery is not showy. It is getting up and doing the work. You do that.

Your smile is contagious. If someone is upset, your smile makes it hard to stay upset. You support friends, listen, and step in when someone needs help. That kind of loyalty is rare.

Chapter 7 — Study buddy & teamwork

Class 11 and 12 — the real years

We shared important years. Those exam months, late-night doubts, quick explanations before tests. You helped with studies and also kept the mood light when stress was high. You were there. That matters.

Thank you for the notes, the explanations, and the small pushes when I needed them.

Chapter 9 — Small thank yous

Because you actually showed up

Good friendships are rare. We found one. Keep it.

Happy birthday Niharika. From your friend who will always call you Temdi and Malich and still expect you to help with physics notes.
